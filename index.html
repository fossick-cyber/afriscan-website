The Problem

Across Africa, communities encroach on pipeline corridors, mining concessions, powerline routes, and telecom sites. By the time you find out, it's a legal dispute, a safety risk, or a multi-million dollar relocation.

🏡

Community Encroachment

New buildings, settlements, and farming creep into your corridor or concession. Without continuous monitoring, encroachment goes undetected until it's too late to act.

💰

Costly Disputes

Undetected land use changes lead to compensation claims, legal battles, and project delays. Early detection saves millions in relocation and litigation costs.

⚠

Safety & Compliance Risk

People living or farming near pipelines, powerlines, and mine sites face real danger. Regulators hold you accountable for what happens in your corridor.

What AfriScan Does

Continuous land use monitoring powered by live satellite imagery, drone surveys, and AI — so you see encroachment as it happens, not months later.

01

One-Click Corridor Scan

Draw a pipeline route on the map or upload a KML, click scan — AfriScan does everything automatically. Fetches high-resolution satellite imagery, runs AI detection, and delivers a complete encroachment report in minutes.

02

Multi-Model AI Detection

Three detection engines work together: Google Open Buildings for known structures, RAMP deep learning for additional buildings, and custom-trained YOLO models fine-tuned on your specific terrain. Catches mud huts, zinc-roof compounds, and informal structures that generic models miss.

03

Risk Segment Analysis

Your pipeline is automatically divided into 500m segments, each classified as high, medium, or low risk based on building density. Interactive maps show colour-coded risk zones — pinpoint exactly where encroachment is worst and prioritise action.

04

Professional Encroachment Reports

Executive summary with overall risk rating, building counts within 50m and 100m buffer zones, satellite imagery crops of every structure with GPS coordinates, and risk segment tables. PDF reports ready for regulators, stakeholders, and legal teams.

05

Historical Comparison

Run the same corridor monthly or quarterly. AfriScan tracks what changed — new buildings, removed structures, settlement expansion. Compare any two scans to see exactly where encroachment is growing over time.

06

Google Earth & GIS Export

Download results as KMZ for Google Earth, GeoPackage, GeoJSON, or Shapefile. Every building includes GPS coordinates, distance to pipeline, buffer zone, and confidence score. Interactive web maps with toggleable risk overlays.

07

Drone Survey Integration

When satellite flags high-risk areas, deploy Afridrone teams for centimetre-resolution verification. Raw drone photos are processed into georeferenced orthophotos directly in AfriScan via WebODM integration.

08

Mobile-Ready Dashboard

Full web dashboard works on any device — desktop, tablet, or phone. Draw routes, run scans, review results, and label training data from the field. No app install required.

How It Works

1

Draw or Upload Your Route

Draw your pipeline route directly on the satellite map, upload a KML/GeoJSON file, or select from existing routes. Set your buffer zones (50m, 100m) and click scan.

2

AI Scans the Corridor

AfriScan automatically fetches high-resolution satellite imagery along your route, runs multiple AI detection models, identifies every building and homestead within your buffer zones, and classifies each pipeline segment by risk level.

3

Review Interactive Results

Explore results on an interactive map with colour-coded risk segments, toggleable building layers, and satellite imagery. Click any detection to see its distance to the pipeline, GPS coordinates, and satellite photo.

4

Download Reports & Take Action

Download a professional PDF report with executive summary, risk analysis, and building photos. Export to Google Earth (KMZ) or GIS formats. Compare with previous scans to track changes over time.
